Mathematics – Year 1

Aligned with the Australian Curriculum intent, grouped by familiar strands.

Strands

Number strand

What students learn

  • Count forwards/backwards to at least 120; skip count by 2s, 5s and 10s.
  • Understand place value: tens and ones; model numbers with MAB or bundling sticks.
  • Add and subtract within 20 fluently; develop strategies (make-10, doubles/near-doubles).
  • Solve addition/subtraction stories within 100 using drawings and materials.
  • Share and group small collections equally; connect to ‘fair shares’.

Classroom activities

  • Daily warm-ups: number line hops, make-10 challenges, doubles/near-doubles flash.
  • Story problems with counters and drawings; record number sentences.
  • Coin shop: make 20c/50c/$1 in different ways.

Algebra strand

What students learn

  • Recognise, describe and continue simple patterns.
  • Use skip counting by 2s, 5s and 10s.

Classroom activities

  • Pattern blocks or beads to create AB/ABB/AAB patterns.
  • Clap and step skip counting games.

Measurement strand

What students learn

  • Measure and compare lengths, masses and capacities using informal units.
  • Tell time to the half-hour.

Classroom activities

  • Use paperclips or blocks to measure classroom objects.
  • Make a class timetable with clocks showing o’clock and half-past.

Space strand

What students learn

  • Recognise and classify 2D shapes and 3D objects.
  • Give and follow simple directions.

Classroom activities

  • Shape hunts around the school; draw shapes seen.
  • Treasure maps with positional language (left, right, near, far).

Statistics strand

What students learn

  • Collect, sort and display data using lists, tables and picture graphs.
  • Answer yes/no questions from class surveys.

Classroom activities

  • Survey classmates on favourite fruit, graph results.
  • Sort objects (buttons, shells) into groups and compare counts.

Probability strand

What students learn

  • Identify everyday events that will happen, won’t happen, or might happen.
  • Use simple language of chance.

Classroom activities

  • Sort events into sure/possible/impossible jars.
  • Toss a coin 10 times; predict heads vs tails.

Teaching sequence

Suggested 10-week outline (Year 1)

Weeks 1–2
Number fluency, warm-ups, diagnostic checks.
Weeks 3–4
Focus strand A + short investigations.
Weeks 5–6
Focus strand B + practice routines.
Weeks 7–8
Focus strand C + problem-solving.
Weeks 9–10
Revision, projects, and simple assessment.

Assessment

Quick Check (5 items)

  1. Skip count: start at 30, count by 5s to 80.
  2. Make 37 with tens and ones blocks; explain how many tens/ones.
  3. Find the total: 8 + 7 (show two strategies).
  4. Read a clock showing 3:30; draw the hands for 9:30.
  5. Graph: “How many more students chose soccer than basketball?”
