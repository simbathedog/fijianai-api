Mathematics – Year 2

Aligned with the Australian Curriculum intent, grouped by familiar strands.

Strands

Number strand

What students learn

  • Count, read and write numbers to at least 1,000; order and locate on a number line.
  • Understand place value to hundreds; partition numbers in different ways.
  • Add and subtract within 100 efficiently; extend to 1,000 with written/mental strategies.
  • Represent multiplication as equal groups and repeated addition (2s, 3s, 5s, 10s); relate division to sharing and grouping.
  • Identify odd and even numbers.

Classroom activities

  • Place-value trades with MAB: bundle 10 ones → 1 ten; 10 tens → 1 hundred.
  • Open number lines: jump by 10s/5s to solve addition/subtraction.
  • Array building with counters: show 3×5, 5×3; read as ‘groups of’.
  • Odd/even hunt around the room; sort number cards quickly.

Algebra strand

What students learn

  • Describe and continue increasing/decreasing number patterns (steps of 2, 3, 5, 10).
  • Find the unknown in simple addition/subtraction equations (e.g., ☐ + 37 = 55).

Classroom activities

  • Rule machines: ‘add 5’, ‘subtract 2’; students generate and explain outputs.
  • Pattern number lines with pegs; predict the next three terms and justify.

Measurement strand

What students learn

  • Measure and compare length using centimetres and metres; select appropriate units.
  • Estimate/measure mass and capacity; read simple scales.
  • Tell time to the quarter-hour on analogue and digital clocks; use calendar language.

Classroom activities

  • Centimetre tape hunt: measure objects, record and order lengths.
  • Quarter-hour time bingo and daily calendar updates.

Space strand

What students learn

  • Classify and draw 2D shapes; identify features of 3D objects.
  • Recognise line symmetry; investigate flips, slides and turns on shapes.
  • Follow and give simple map directions.

Classroom activities

  • Symmetry art with mirrors/folding; label lines of symmetry.
  • Grid maps: write and follow step-by-step routes to a target.

Statistics strand

What students learn

  • Collect and organise class data; make column and picture graphs.
  • Interpret graphs using ‘most’, ‘least’, ‘how many more/fewer’.
  • Understand that data answers a question about a group.

Classroom activities

  • Class survey (favourite sport): tally → table → column graph.
  • Compare two categories: ‘How many more chose A than B?’

Probability strand

What students learn

  • Use chance language: certain, likely, unlikely, impossible.
  • Trial simple events (spinners/coins/dice) and compare results with expectations.

Classroom activities

  • Unequal spinner investigation: predict the most likely colour and test.
  • Coin-toss runs of 20; discuss streaks vs. long-run balance.

Teaching sequence

Suggested 10-week outline (Year 2)

Weeks 1–2
Number fluency & diagnostic checks.
Weeks 3–4
Focus strand A + investigations.
Weeks 5–6
Focus strand B + practice routines.
Weeks 7–8
Focus strand C + problem-solving.
Weeks 9–10
Revision, projects, light assessment.

Assessment

Quick Check (5 items)

  1. Place 846 on a number line between 800 and 900.
  2. Solve: 58 + 27 (show a jumps-on-the-line or make-ten strategy).
  3. Draw an array for 3 × 4 and write the related division facts.
  4. Read the time shown: quarter past 7; draw quarter to 3.
  5. From the graph, how many more students chose bikes than buses?
