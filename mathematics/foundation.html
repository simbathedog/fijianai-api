Strands

Number strand

What students learn

  • Recognise, model, read and write numbers 0–20; connect numerals, words and quantities.
  • Count forwards/backwards to 20; subitise small collections (to 5).
  • Compare collections: more/less/same; order small numbers on a number track.
  • Join and separate collections in practical situations (early addition/subtraction).
  • Partition small numbers in different ways (e.g., 5 as 2 and 3).

Classroom activities

  • Counting circles; numeral–quantity matching cards to 20.
  • Build-and-break with counters or tens frames; tell the ‘story’ of each change.
  • Make 5 and 10 in different ways with connecting cubes.

Algebra strand

What students learn

  • Sort and classify objects by one attribute (colour, size, shape).
  • Copy, continue and create simple repeating patterns (AB, AAB, ABB).
  • Use the language of equal and not equal with materials.

Classroom activities

  • Pattern necklaces: AB / AAB with beads; students extend and create their own.
  • Sorting stations: sort by one rule, classmates guess the rule.

Measurement strand

What students learn

  • Compare length, mass and capacity using direct comparison and informal units.
  • Order events and use time language: before/after, morning/afternoon; days of the week.
  • Become aware of temperature and duration in everyday contexts.

Classroom activities

  • Measure with footsteps, paper clips, and cups; record which is longer/heavier/holds more.
  • Picture schedule of the day; place activities in order.

Space strand

What students learn

  • Identify common 2D shapes (circle, triangle, square, rectangle) and describe features informally.
  • Handle 3D objects (sphere, cube, cylinder, cone) and link to real items.
  • Use position and direction language: in, on, under, between; forward, backward, turn.

Classroom activities

  • Shape hunt around the classroom and school; photo wall.
  • Robot routes: give simple turn/step instructions on a floor grid.

Statistics strand

What students learn

  • Collect simple yes/no or choice data as tally or pictures.
  • Create and read a basic pictograph about the class.

Classroom activities

  • Class vote: favourite fruit; make a picture graph and discuss ‘most’ and ‘least’.

Probability strand

What students learn

  • Use chance language: will happen, won’t happen, might happen.
  • Explore everyday events as certain/possible/impossible.

Classroom activities

  • Bag of coloured cubes with mostly one colour: predict and test which colour you’ll likely pull.

Teaching sequence

Suggested 10-week outline (Foundation)

Weeks 1–2
Number fluency, warm-ups, diagnostic checks.
Weeks 3–4
Focus strand A + short investigations.
Weeks 5–6
Focus strand B + practice routines.
Weeks 7–8
Focus strand C + problem-solving.
Weeks 9–10
Revision, projects, and simple assessment.

Assessment

Quick Check (5 items)

  1. Show a tens frame with 7. Ask: “How many? How many more to make 10?”
  2. Place 3 objects, then 5. Ask: “Which is more? How do you know?”
  3. Extend a bead pattern ABAB… What comes next?
  4. Point to shapes: circle, triangle, square — name them.
  5. Chance: “Will the sun rise tomorrow?” (certain / possible / impossible)
